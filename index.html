Discovery and Prospection

Altough minning asteroids has long been considered science-fiction, it is a true possibility nowadays. Hence, it is imperative that we be able to determine suitable asteoids to mine. With more than 20,000 near-Earth objects (NEOs) larger than 100m diameter, minning asteroids can become a profitable industry.

Three stages must be accomplished

  • Discovery -> Asteroids large enough to be mined
  • Remote characterization -> Spectroscopic and Photometric techniques
  • Local characterization -> Sent spacecraft close to a NEO

Discovery and Prospection

For astronomers a "color" represents the difference between two photometric bands. As it was demonstrated by the "Eight-Color Asteroid Survey", it is possible to identify a taxonomy of asteroids types with that data. Besides, closer images given by local characterization techniques may offer us a localization of potential sources of minerals on the asteroid.

Model and mapping

After establishing the minning potential of an asteroid, it is fundamental to send probes to this celestial body for exploratory purposes. The main mission of these probes is to set targets that are easy to reach and are suitable for minning purposes.

We propose that the exploratory probes not only search for potential targets, but also mine the sources of mineral. Through Voronoi partions, we optimize the communication coverage and the localization of extraction units.

Model and mapping

With the mineral resources targeted, the next step is to set the motion planning for the exploratory/minning units. Our first aproximation is carried out through potential fields. The minning targets are initially considered as attractors and the gathering points as repulsors. After the ore is collected, the minning targets are repulsors and the gathering points are attractors. Therefore, the motion trajectories change dynamically.
